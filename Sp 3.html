India's Language Philosophy â€” The Core Idea
India did not impose a single national language. Instead it built a constitutional architecture of practical bilingualism at the Centre (Hindi + English), linguistic freedom for States, English dominance in judiciary, and protective safeguards for minorities.
The 8th Schedule provides cultural recognition. Classical status is separate. Together they form the world's most linguistically diverse democracy's constitutional language framework.
